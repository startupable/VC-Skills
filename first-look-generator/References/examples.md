## Example 1: Goodbill (Seed, $5M)

**Header:**
- DATE: 7/15/25
- COMPANY: Goodbill
- HQ: Seattle, WA
- STAGE: Seed
- INVESTMENT SOUGHT: $5M
- REACH CONTACT: CM, JH, SG
- TEAM: Patrick & Ian

**PRODUCT DESCRIPTION:**
An AI engine finding discounts and errors in bills before an employer-funded plan pays a hospital. The initial wedge is finding 501(r) discounts (a requirement at non-profit hospitals) on behalf of TPAs.

**IMPACT THESIS:**
Fair (and lower) hospital bills for plans and patients.

**UNFAIR ADVANTAGE:**
Moat is in the "processing power," meaning they digitized and structured the data and then put it within the right workflow. Highly sticky once integrated.

**PROS:**
- **Traction**
  - $6M ARR contracted, $1.2M run rate, 29 plan admins under contract, $30M in pipeline and 116% NDR
  - 60% of patients engage
- **Unique & efficient wedge**
  - Unique 501(r) wedge fully digitizing discounts at 3.5k hospitals
  - This has led to 3x faster deals
  - 4 month sales cycles and 70% close rate
- **Clear ROI for customers**
  - The engine drives 4x more discounts and significantly faster error correction
  - Yielding 12% plan savings on average
- **Massive but overlooked market**
  - Healthcare billing is notoriously complex
  - Hospital bills are the #1 source of medical debt in US
  - More venture dollars have gone to RCM on the provider side and TPAs have been quite overlooked
- **Consumer validation for B2B company**
  - Originally started with the consumer and the product work but when first TPA reached out it was obvious they could reach meaningful scale that path

**CONS:**
- Late in the fundraising process and expecting term sheets by the end of this week or early next
- Previously raised $5M
- Want to make sure product velocity is high
- LT vision as "AI savings infrastructure" is not clear to us exactly what that means
- Founders have been co-founders are 13 years together and are self-proclaimed workflow automation junkies but do not have deep healthcare roots or motivation

**RECOMMENDATIONS:** 2nd pass through the data room

## Example 4: Marit (Series A, $8M)

**Header:**
- DATE: 9/23/25
- COMPANY: Marit
- HQ: SF / remote
- STAGE: Series A
- INVESTMENT SOUGHT: $8M
- REACH CONTACT: SG, JH, CM
- TEAM: Vikas Sabnani (CEO), Tim Anderson (CPO), Owen Tran (CTO)

**PRODUCT DESCRIPTION:**
Marit is a marketplace where healthcare professionals can share salaries (initial wedge), find jobs, and connect with peers in an anonymous but verified community. It is the next gen of Doximity.

**IMPACT THESIS:**
Marit makes medicine more transparent and fair by giving clinicians access to real pay data, better job options, and an honest peer community.

**UNFAIR ADVANTAGE:**
The team built Glassdoor and is applying the same UGC model and initial salary wedge to healthcare.

**PROS:**
- The founding team includes super relevant product expertise (Glassdoor) and industry expertise (practicing doctors).
  - Tim and Vikas built and scaled Glassdoor's core data science and recruiting products, giving them a proven playbook for UGC-driven marketplaces.
  - The team has a practicing anesthesiologist, grounding the product in day-to-day clinical realities.
  - They've assembled much of the original Glassdoor leadership (product, engineering, growth).
- The product that has been built so far (salary & jobs) is really thoughtful and 10x better than Doximity
  - "Jobs" product goes far beyond a standard listings board: AI standardizes job descriptions, adds >30 filters (academic vs outpatient, call requirements, comp, schedule), and surfaces a "fit score" based on things like commute time.
  - Balances anonymity with verification so users can post honestly while employers and peers know they're real clinicians.
  - Built with clear extensibility where each concentric product (salaries → jobs → news → community) expands engagement.
- Early traction and enthusiasm from users is quite promising.
  - The sub-reddit for medicine has 236K weekly visitors so clearly there is a demand for some kind of community with anonymity.
  - 48% of surveyed users would be very disappointed if Marit went away.
  - Growth to 100k users projected in under 18 months, fueled 55% by word of mouth alone.
- Doximity is one of the best ever performing healthcare stocks, yet, it is ripe for disruption.
  - 80% of Doximity's revenue still comes from syndicated newsfeed ads, with limited content network effects.
  - Marit combines the engagement of Reddit with the verified data model of Glassdoor.
  - Doximity has shown there are large, proven monetization categories: recruitment ads, locums staffing, and pharma ads.

**CONS:**
- The initial wedge of salary transparency is a one-time use case, not something with recurring engagement.
- Can they get the marketplace flywheel off the ground? Can they translate the static salary transparency wedge to an actual engaging, monetized community.
- Building an ad business relies on finding and sustaining high engagement.
- Doximity added workflow tools which may be the reason users come back to it. Marit does not plan to go this route, but is open to it if critical.
- Requires hundreds of thousands of clinicians to reach meaningful revenue.
- Salary is the only part of the product currently live. Jobs, news, and community are going live between now and January '26, meaning we have little information for this round on what engagement will look like.
- Need to move users from established players like Doximity, Reddit and FB groups.
- Round dynamics and stage
  - This will be a Series A bet at seed level traction
  - It will primarily be a bet on team + market

**RECOMMENDATIONS:** Continued diligence

## Example 5: Bem AI (Seed, $1-2M)

**Header:**
- DATE: 6/9/25
- COMPANY: Bem (deck)
- HQ: NY
- STAGE: Seed
- INVESTMENT SOUGHT: $1-2M
- REACH CONTACT: EC, JK
- FOUNDER: Upal Saha, Antonio Bustamante

**PRODUCT DESCRIPTION:**
Bem is an API and email gateway that transforms unstructured data (PDFs, Emails, Text, Voicemails, WhatsApp) into production-grade data

**IMPACT THESIS:**
Unlocking AI adoption for old school, regulated industries like healthcare, supply chain, finance services, etc. where data mess and complexity slows down implementation

**UNFAIR ADVANTAGE:**
Owning the data workflows and evaluation systems

**PROS:**
- **Team**
  - Antonio is experienced and technical. Previously founded 3 companies:
    - Healthcare tech company in college
    - Kyte: Built neural networks on Python's AST for code completion (10 years ahead of market)
    - Silo: Food supply chain vertical ERP and fintech platform (backed by a16z, Initialized, Haystack)
- **Problem**
  - Supply chain/enterprise companies receive 5-10k orders daily through unstructured formats: PDFs, Emails, Text, Voicemails, WhatsApp
  - Each order takes 3-30 minutes to manually input into systems
  - The team felt this pain point at their previous company
- **Product & Market**
  - Uses model orchestration engine to determine best model for each document type
  - 93-94% accuracy with zero-shot/no training
  - Can reach 99.999% with labeled data
  - Built-in evaluation workflows (human in the loop and customer-led)
- **Go-to-market**
  - Vertical focus on supply chain, other verticals through inbound
  - Onboarding takes less than 1 week for standard customers
  - Planning public launch in June for self-serve capability
- **Traction**
  - $270k ARR, 5x growth since Q1
  - 7-12 customers, ACVs reaching $30-50k range
  - Notable customers include Carrot Fertility and Ryder Logistics
- **Fundraising**
  - Raised $3.7M fifteen months ago at $17M post-money valuation
  - Currently raising $1-2M extension
  - Existing investors likely to participate

**CONS:**
- **Risks**
  - Price and distribution competition from hyperscalers solutions
  - AWS Textract and Google Document AI stop at extraction
  - Bem doesn't just extract text: it handles document splitting, classification, routing, validation, and schema mapping end-to-end specifically for each customer
  - Hyperscalers only improve global models Bem auto-evaluates every new model against customer-specific ground truth and hot-swaps the best model
  - Hyperscalers are cloud-first; Bem offers VPC and will offer on-prem deployment (Q3) for healthcare, finance, and EU clients with compliance needs

**RECOMMENDATIONS:** Second look
